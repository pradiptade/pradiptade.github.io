Overview

Current projects in the lab are along two themes.

  • (Mobile) Application design using Machine Learning: We explore use of data driven techniques for designing novel applications, such as recognizing emotions from smartphone activities, or student attention levels in classroom based on screen activity. We also work on analysis of traffic from mobile devices, especially video traffic.
  • Design and Application of Mobile Edge Computing: Mobile Edge Computing (MEC) is a paradigm where remote resources can be harnessed to seamlessly augment the capabilities of a mobile device. The remote resource can be within the local area network, or reside in the cloud within large data centers. Use of MEC can help in designing novel applications by allowing enhanced resources, but it may come at a cost of higher latency. Our objective is to explore the space of novel applications, without degrading the user experience due to the use of networked resources.

Project List

  • Mobile Affective Computing

    Ability to detect the emotion states of an individual to different contextual and environmental stimuli has numerous applications. Affective computing relates to capturing emotions of an individual based on cues, like activity patterns, interaction patterns, physiological data from wearable devices. With growing use of mobile devices, data sources that help to recognize mental states, or emotions, of individuals are increasing. Wearable devices and smartphones, which are becoming ubiquitous in our lives, will be valuable source of data for diagnosis and even treatment of mental health disorders, like depression, bipolar disease, and rehabilitation from addictions. We investigate novel techniques for emotion recognition, specially focusing on the use of mobile and wearable devices for data collection, and improving the accuracy of detection by intelligently combining different data sources.

  • Indoor/Outdoor Locationing

    Several approaches in locationing systems, both indoor and outdoor, explore the use of fingerprints hidden in the user's environment to identify a location. Images, and image features, constitute one of the natural fingerprints of a location. Cues inside an image, like landmarks, are matched against a database of geotagged images for locationing, but the process is compute and time intensive.In this work, we present the use of texts in the images, to pinpoint a user's location. The key intuition behind this work is that the collection of names of businesses, appearing in an image forms a bag-of-words that provides a unique signature for a location. Typically, use of OCR in outdoor settings is considered a hard problem, specially on resource constrained smartphones. We design a system, called WhereAmI, which presents a practical solution to the OCR problem. A fast bag-of-words matching technique, that can handle errors in text detection, is designed for locationing. We have implemented WhereAmI on Android based smartphones and evaluated the locationing accuracy under different settings in multilingual environment.

  • Real-time Application Support in Mobile Cloud

    The mobile edge/cloud paradigm promises to augment resource limited mobile devices by providing unlimited compute power. Offloading part of the computation of an application not only saves battery power, but also speeds up the computation. However, selecting the appropriate portions for offloading is challenging, specially under different constraints, like variable and unreliable wireless channel, tight real-time deadlines to complete tasks. In this project, we are exploring the design space of mobile cloud computing systems, with a focus on real-time applications, like video streaming.
